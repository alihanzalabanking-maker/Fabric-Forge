Got it — let me explain how visitors actually cost you credits.

How visitors eat your credits
When someone opens your app, two small things happen that nibble at your credits:

1. The server "wakes up" and sends them the app
Every time a visitor opens your link, Replit's server has to:

Stay turned on (waiting for visitors)
Send the app's files to their phone/computer
This is called compute (the server doing work). It costs a tiny fraction of a cent per visitor.

2. Data travels from the server to the visitor
The app's files (text, images, code) travel through the internet to the visitor's phone. This is called bandwidth or network transfer.

For your MealPrep app, opening it once = roughly 1 MB of data sent.
Replit charges around $0.10 per GB of data sent out (1 GB = 1,000 MB).

Real numbers for your app
Let's say your app weighs about 1 MB to load:

Visitors per month	Data sent	Credit cost
100 visitors	100 MB	~€0.01 (1 cent)
1,000 visitors	1 GB	~€0.10 (10 cents)
10,000 visitors	10 GB	~€1.00
100,000 visitors	100 GB	~€10.00
Plus the server staying on 24/7 costs around €3–€5/month no matter how many visitors come.

So what you really pay each month
For a small app like MealPrep with normal use:

Server running all month: ~€4
A few hundred visitors: ~€0.05
Total: ~€4 out of your €25 credits ✅
You'd have €21 left for asking me to make changes, or for traffic to grow.
